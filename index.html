Contraindications to permanent makeup treatment

Before undergoing permanent makeup treatment, please read the contraindications to the procedure

Permanent eyebrow makeup:

  • Pregnancy
  • Lactation period
  • Allergy to hair dyes
  • Lidocaine allergy
  • Allergy to a relaxing preparation that gives an anesthetic effect, used in some cases
  • Dermatological skin lesions in the treated area (purulent acne, purulent conditions, allergic or fungal lesions in the treated areas, hemangiomas, lichens, papillomas, epidermal discontinuities, sunburn)
  • Skin that is difficult to heal or tends to develop colloids and scars
  • Psoriasis (in active phase)
  • Unstable diabetes
  • Haemophilia
  • Cancer
  • Jaundice
  • HIV
  • Cardiac arrhythmia
  • Epilepsy
  • Nervous hyperactivity, nervous tics
  • Albinism
  • Active herpes
  • Autoimmune disease
  • Steroid treatment
  • Antibiotic therapy
  • Blood thinning medications
  • Medicines for local application in the treated area
  • The epidermis exfoliation treatment was carried out for a period of 4 weeks before the treatment
  • Using a conditioner to revitalize and stimulate eyebrow growth a month before the procedure
  • Injecting the forehead area with preparations intended to immobilize the frontal muscles during the week before the procedure (BTX, Botox, Azzalure)
  • Staying in very sunny places in the coming days
  • Increased temperature and cold on the day of the procedure
  • Inflammation of the body on the day of the procedure
  • Consumption of alcohol and drugs in the last 24 hours
  • Nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drugs (NSAIDs)
  • Oily, porous and thick skin (contraindication for the hair/microblading method)

Eyebrows aftercare

  • On the evening of the first day (the day of the treatment), gently cleanse your eyebrows using Phi Wipes without stretching the skin. This way, the excess lymph is removed to prevent the formation of scabs.
  • For the next 6 days, you don't need to clean your eyebrows because the lymph will not be coming out any longer
  • If you have dry skin, apply a very thin layer of after cream from me once a day.
  • If you have normal skin, apply a very thin layer of after cream one more time after 4-5 days (once the eyebrows start to itch and peel off).
  • If you have oily skin, you do not need to apply any cream.
  • IN THE FIRST 7 DAYS AVOID
    • All creams (except the protective ones provided by a specialist) and any makeup in the eyebrow area.
    • Exercise that causes excessive sweating, sauna, swimming pools and all facial treatments.
    • Do not pick any scabs. Allow it to fall off naturally.
    • Once the scabs fall off, the pigment colour will almost disappear and appear gradually during the first 2-3 weeks. Your brows may look up to 50% lighter.
  • IN THE FIRST 30 DAYS AVOID
    • Sun tanning, sun beds, light (laser) therapy, chemical peels, fruit acids, microdermabrasion,
    • Avoid laser treatments over the treated area during the healing period (Fraxel laser, IPL).
    • Protect your eyebrows from the sun by using sun protection cream - minimum SPF 30 (but do not use it during the first 7 days).

Lips aftercare

  • DAY OF THE TREATMENT
    • During the first day (day of the treatment), gently, without rubbing, cleanse your lips with Phi Wipes Remove the lymph to prevent the creation of bigger scabs. When your lips are clean and dry, apply the after cream provided by the specialist.
    • This cream can be applied anytime you feel a tightening of the lips in the following few days (usually up to 7 days).
  • IN THE FIRST 7 DAYS AVOID
    • Sauna, swimming pools, gym, make-up in the lips area, sun tanning, cosmetic treatments.
    • Absolutely do not pick any scabs. Allow it to fall off naturally.
    • To preserve the colour for as long as possible, use sun protection when sun tanning (minimum 30 SPF).
